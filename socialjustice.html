Mental Illness

A large percentage of the homeless population in Los Angeles suffer from severe mental illnesses or disabilities. These disorders lead to behavioral problems that make it harder for people to carry out daily activities and maintain a normal lifestyle. They push away their family and friends who could offer a helping hand. As a result of these factors and the stresses of living with a mental disorder, people with mental illnesses are much more likely to become homeless than people without. People are living on the street because of something they cannot control. (Source: National Coalition for the Homeless)
